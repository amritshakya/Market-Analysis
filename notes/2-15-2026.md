America’s wealthiest universities have been grappling with subpar returns on their private-capital investments.  Harvard says cashing out of some private-market investments early is now part of a long-term strategy.

Private equity has long counted America’s wealthiest universities among its largest and most-loyal clients. But the market for private-company investments has gotten crowded, and returns now struggle to match broader stock-market benchmarks.

The share of 26-year-olds who have transferred funds to investment accounts since turning 22 as of May 2025, up sharply from 8% in 2015, according to data from the JPMorgan Chase Institute. The numbers don’t include people investing in 401(k)s. Locked out of home ownership, a younger generation of investors is instead trying to build wealth by putting money into the stock market.
