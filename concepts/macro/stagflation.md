## Stagflation

### What it is
A macro regime where growth slows while inflation remains elevated. This breaks the usual relationship where inflation falls when growth weakens.

### Why it matters
- Limits Fed flexibility (can’t cut easily, can’t hike aggressively)
- Creates tension between supporting growth vs controlling inflation
- Often leads to higher volatility across assets

### How it shows up in markets
- Bonds sell off (inflation risk)
- Equities struggle (growth risk)
- Commodities outperform (supply-driven inflation)
- Policy uncertainty increases

### Current relevance (2026)
Oil-driven inflation has pushed CPI higher while housing and sentiment are weakening. This creates a mild stagflation setup where policy support is constrained.

### My takeaway
Stagflation is not just “bad macro” — it’s a regime where **policy stops helping markets**, which is what makes it dangerous.
