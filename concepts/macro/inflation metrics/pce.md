## PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures)

### What it is
The Fed’s preferred inflation measure.

### Why it matters
- Used for policy decisions
- Smoother and more comprehensive than CPI

### Key differences from CPI
- Dynamic basket (accounts for substitution)
- Broader coverage
- Lower housing weight

### How it shows up in markets
- Drives Fed expectations more than CPI (medium-term)
- Less volatile, more policy-relevant

### Current relevance (2026)
PCE remains around ~2.8–3%, not low enough for rate cuts, reinforcing Fed’s “wait” stance.

### My takeaway
PCE is **what inflation actually is for policy**, while CPI is what markets react to.
