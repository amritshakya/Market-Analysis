## CPI (Consumer Price Index)

### What it is
Measures the price of a fixed basket of consumer goods and services.

### Why it matters
- Most visible inflation metric
- Drives short-term market reactions
- Strong influence on yields and equity sentiment

### Key limitation
- Fixed basket → does not reflect substitution behavior
- More volatile and backward-looking

### How it shows up in markets
- Higher CPI → yields rise, equities pressured
- Immediate reaction metric

### Current relevance (2026)
March CPI rose to ~3.3% YoY, driven largely by energy. Confirms inflation re-acceleration.

### My takeaway
CPI tells you **what just happened to inflation**, not where it’s going.
